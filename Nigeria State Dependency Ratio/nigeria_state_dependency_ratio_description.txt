TITLE
  Nigeria Living Standards Survey - 2018/2019: Dependency Ratio by State

OVERVIEW
  This dataset provides state-level dependency ratio estimates
  for all 36 states of Nigeria plus the Federal Capital
  Territory (FCT), derived from the Nigeria Living Standards
  Survey (NLSS) 2018/2019. The dependency ratio measures the
  number of dependants (children and elderly) relative to the
  working-age population in each state.

  A ratio above 1.0 indicates more dependants than workers,
  while a ratio below 1.0 indicates more workers than
  dependants. It is a key socioeconomic indicator used in
  labour market analysis, welfare planning, and transport
  demand modelling.

INTENDED USE
  - K-means / clustering analysis of Nigerian states by
    socioeconomic profile
  - Labour market and welfare policy research
  - Transport demand and mode choice modelling inputs
  - Spatial visualisation and choropleth mapping

LIMITATIONS
  - Point-in-time estimate from the 2018/2019 survey cycle;
    may not reflect current conditions
  - State averages mask significant within-state variation
    across LGAs, urban and rural zones
  - Dependency ratio definition follows NBS methodology;
    may differ from World Bank or ILO conventions
