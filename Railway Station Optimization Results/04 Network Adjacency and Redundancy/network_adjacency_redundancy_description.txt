TITLE
  Nigeria State Capital Adjacency Matrices and
  Station Redundancy Zone Identification

OVERVIEW
  Three datasets encoding spatial proximity relationships
  between state capitals at the geographic (border-sharing)
  and functional (HSR-proximity) levels, plus identified
  redundancy zones where nearby station pairs would
  duplicate coverage.

HOW THEY WERE GENERATED
  adjacency_matrix_geographic.csv  (37×37 binary)
    Value = 1 if the two states share a land border;
    0 otherwise. Derived from official Nigerian state
    border topology data.

  adjacency_matrix_functional.csv  (37×37 binary)
    Value = 1 if capitals are within 150 km OR within
    60 minutes at 250 km/h HSR speed; 0 otherwise.
    Computed from distance_matrix.csv and hsr_time_matrix.csv.

  redundancy_zones.csv  (10 pairs)
    Capital pairs with inter-city distance < 100 km,
    flagged as potential station redundancy risks. Includes
    a flag indicating whether the pair are also geographic
    neighbours.

PURPOSE
  Geographic adjacency guided zone-balance constraints
  (minimum 1 station per geopolitical zone). Functional
  adjacency defined the λ redundancy penalty in the
  modified K-means objective function. Redundancy zones
  informed station spacing enforcement (80 km minimum).

VIEWING ORDER
