TITLE
  Stage 1 Preliminary Audit, Configuration, and Network
  Sharing Rule Files

OVERVIEW
  Six support files produced during Stage 1 data preparation.
  These capture the preliminary data audit findings, objective
  function weight presets, Stage 1 completion state, spacing
  constraint violations, and station service sharing rules
  for the Nigerian state capital network.

HOW EACH FILE WAS GENERATED

  data_summary_report.json  (step0.py)
    Output of the initial data audit script that loaded and
    assessed all 8 raw source datasets before integration.
    Records which datasets loaded successfully, how many
    capitals and traffic routes were identified, and flags
    that duplicate state name variants (e.g. "Anambra" vs
    "Anambra State") needed cleaning — producing 44 apparent
    capitals that were later cleaned to 37.

  objective_weights.json  (step1others.py)
    Three preset weight configurations for the Stage 2
    objective function:
      cost_focused  : α=0.7, β=0.3 (minimise network length)
      balanced      : α=0.5, β=0.5 (equal priority)
      access_focused: α=0.3, β=0.7 (maximise accessibility)
    These are applied in combination with λ (redundancy
    penalty) during the modified K-means sweep.

  stage1_summary.json  (step1others.py)
    Machine-readable completion record for all Stage 1
    deliverables: lists output files, data quality metrics
    (coverage, null counts, actual vs estimated distances),
    and confirms readiness for Stage 2.

  scenario1_spacing_violations.csv  (step1others.py)
    3 capital pairs that violate the 80 km minimum
    station spacing constraint under Scenario 1 (all capitals
    have stations). These pairs are geographically close and
    represent redundancy risks. Identical to the
    redundancy_zones.csv content but computed independently
    via the constraint checker.
    Pairs: Umuahia ↔ Owerri (66.4 km) | Awka ↔ Enugu (70.2 km) | Uyo ↔ Calabar (58.5 km)

  station_sharing_rules.json  (step1_5_redundancy.py)
    For each of 36 potential hub stations: lists which
    other capitals fall within its 150 km functional service
    zone and could be served without a dedicated station.
    Used to inform the "max distance to station" constraint
    in Scenario 2 (≤150 km) and to identify which non-station
    capitals could be served by nearby HSR stops.

  multi_served_capitals.json  (step1_5_redundancy.py)
    34 of 37 capitals that lie within the
    functional zone (≤150 km) of 2 or more other capitals,
    meaning they could potentially be served by multiple
    neighbouring stations. This informs the redundancy
    penalty formulation — placing stations too close creates
    overlapping service zones.

PURPOSE
  Collectively these files document the Stage 1 decision
  trail: why certain constraints were set, which data quality
  issues were identified, and how the service sharing rules
  were derived. Essential for reproducibility and peer review.

VIEWING ORDER
  Dataset #9 — supplementary to Groups 01–05. Review
  alongside Group 05 (Constraints and Parameters) for full
  context on how constraint values were determined.
