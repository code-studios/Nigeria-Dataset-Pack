TITLE
  Nigeria Inter-City HSR Demand Matrices and Corridor
  Centrality Analysis (Gravity Model)

OVERVIEW
  Four datasets encoding synthetic travel demand between
  Nigerian state capitals, generated using two gravity model
  formulations. These represent potential passenger demand
  on a hypothetical high-speed rail network.

HOW THEY WERE GENERATED
  demand_matrix_basic.csv  (37×37)
    Standard gravity model:
      Demand_ij = (Pop_i × Pop_j) / Distance_ij²
    Uses raw population and Haversine distances.

  demand_matrix_enhanced.csv  (37×37)
    Enhanced gravity model:
      Demand_ij = (Pop_i × Pop_j × Econ_i × Econ_j)
                  / Distance_ij^1.8
    Distance exponent 1.8 is calibrated for HSR (reduces
    distance friction vs standard road models). Economic
    proxy weights are from the master_dataset.

  demand_corridors.csv  (666 rows)
    Flattened upper-triangle of both demand matrices into
    origin-destination pairs, enriched with state names and
    distances. Enables corridor-level ranking and filtering.

  demand_centrality.csv  (37 rows)
    Per-capital sum of all incoming + outgoing enhanced
    demand. Represents each capital's importance as a
    demand hub in the national network.

PURPOSE
  Used to rank station location candidates, prioritize
  high-demand corridors, define mandatory stations (Lagos),
  and evaluate demand capture rate in Scenario 2 results.

VIEWING ORDER
  Dataset #3 — review after the distance matrices.
  Recommended sub-order:
    1. demand_matrix_enhanced.csv (core demand surface)
    2. demand_corridors.csv (top corridor ranking)
    3. demand_centrality.csv (hub importance scores)
    4. demand_matrix_basic.csv (baseline comparison)
