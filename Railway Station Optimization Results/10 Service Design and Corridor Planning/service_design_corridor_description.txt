TITLE
  Nigeria HSR Service Pattern Design, 3-Train Route
  Allocation, and Strategic Corridor Planning

OVERVIEW
  Eight datasets representing three distinct layers of service
  design work built on top of the station optimization results:
    Layer A — S1 network path-based service patterns
              (network_path.py)
    Layer B — S2 15-station 3-train zone-allocated routes,
              preliminary version (network_path2.py)
    Layer C — Strategic 5-corridor plan for S1 network
              (route.py)

  NOTE: The FINAL authoritative 3-train OD-locked service
  design is in Group 08 (train_service_design.json). The
  file s2_3train_routes_preliminary.json in this group is
  an earlier zone-greedy TSP version — retained for
  methodological comparison.

HOW EACH FILE WAS GENERATED

  service_patterns.csv  (15 rows)  (network_path.py)
    Automated path-based service pattern design for S1's
    full 37-station MST network. For each of the top 15
    demand OD pairs + 5 strategic pairs, NetworkX shortest
    paths were computed on the MST graph. Stopping rules
    were then applied by distance and demand tier:
      EXPRESS     : distance > 500 km AND demand > 1e7
      SEMI-EXPRESS: distance > 300 km
      SMART       : distance > 150 km
      LOCAL       : distance ≤ 150 km
    Columns: service_id, origin, destination, service_type,
    demand, distance_km, full_path, stops, num_stops,
    skipped_stations, num_skipped, total_time_hours,
    avg_speed_kmh, daily_frequency.

  service_routes_detailed.csv  (25 rows)  (network_path.py)
    Segment-by-segment breakdown of every stop sequence in
    service_patterns.csv. One row per consecutive station
    pair per service. Columns: service_id, sequence, from,
    to, is_origin, is_destination.

  station_utilization.csv  (13 rows)  (network_path.py)
    Count of how many designed services stop at each station,
    identifying under-utilised stations (<2 services).
    Top station: Ikeja (7 services).

  service_design_summary.json  (network_path.py)
    Aggregate statistics: 15 total services,
    122 total daily trains,
    avg 2.7 stops/service,
    avg 1.1 hours/service,
    13 stations served.
    Service type breakdown:
      EXPRESS: 1  |  SEMI-EXPRESS: 3
      SMART: 3  |  LOCAL: 8

  s2_3train_routes_preliminary.json  (network_path2.py)
    PRELIMINARY 3-train route design for S2's 15-station
    network using zone-pair allocation + greedy nearest-
    neighbour TSP. Stations were grouped by geopolitical
    zone, then 2 zones assigned per train. Routes were
    computed as circular TSP tours within each train's
    allocated stations. This is NOT the final design —
    the final OD-locked version is in Group 08.
    Trains: Train 1: ['South-West', 'South-South'], Train 2: ['North-West', 'North-East'], Train 3: ['North-Central', 'South-East']

  corridor_plan.json  (route.py)
    Strategic 5-corridor plan for the S1 network, defining
    major intercity HSR routes based on geographic and
    economic logic. For each corridor: origin, destination,
    intermediate stops, zones served, distance, travel time,
    and recommended service frequency. Corridors:
      1. Lagos–Abuja–Kano (North-South Spine)
      2. Lagos–Port Harcourt (Southern Coastal)
      3. Abuja–Port Harcourt (Central-South)
      4. Kano–Maiduguri (Northern Belt)
      5. Enugu–Calabar (Eastern)

  corridor_routes.csv  (13 rows)  (route.py)
    Segment-level breakdown of all 5 corridors. One row per
    consecutive city pair per corridor with distances.
    Columns: corridor, from, to, distance_km.

  comparison_summary.json  (step2_1_2.py)
    Compact S1 vs S2 comparison produced at the end of the
    initial optimization run (before full evaluation in
    step2_3_5.py). Records k, network_length_km, coverage_pct,
    and redundancy for both scenarios.
    NOTE: Use corrected_metrics.json (Group 07) for final
    authoritative figures; this file reflects uncorrected
    intermediate values.

PURPOSE
  Documents the full service design exploration beyond
  station placement — demonstrating how the optimized
  networks can be operationalised with concrete train
  services and strategic corridor priorities.

VIEWING ORDER

    1. corridor_plan.json          — strategic 5-corridor plan
    2. corridor_routes.csv         — corridor segment details
    3. service_patterns.csv        — S1 service pattern sweep
